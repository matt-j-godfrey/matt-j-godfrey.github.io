jAG25 – Young German Astronomical Society Summer School

← Back to all events

I attended the 5th Summer School of the junge Astronomische Gesellschaft (jAG), held at the Leibniz Institute for Astrophysics Potsdam (AIP) from June 2–4, 2025. This was a particularly special edition of the summer school, as it marked the first joint jAG/Royal Astronomical Society (RAS) event, bringing together early-career astronomers from both Germany and the UK.

I was fortunate to be awarded a travel grant by the RAS to support my attendance at the meeting; my sincere thanks to them for making the trip possible!

The programme featured a hands-on workshop on Bayesian statistics led by Francesca Capel (Max Planck Institute for Physics), a series of invited science talks, and a set of job talks offering insight into careers in astronomy beyond academia. We also had the chance to tour the AIP, the historic Einstein Tower, and the Great Refractor in Potsdam.

On the final day, I gave a talk at the junge AG Kolloquium titled: “Spatial Mapping of Atmospheric Winds and Sodium Absorption in Exoplanets Using High-Resolution Spectroscopy”, where I presented recent results using code to model transmission spectra of hot Jupiters.

It was a great opportunity to share my work, meet researchers from across Europe, and get inspired by the breadth of current astrophysical research. I’m very grateful to the organisers and the jAG/RAS teams for putting together such a supportive and fun event.
