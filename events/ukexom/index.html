UKEXOM – UK Exoplanet Meeting 2025

← Back to all events

I presented a poster on spatial mapping of atmospheric winds and sodium absorption on HD 189733b using the TERMINATOR code, and received thoughtful feedback that will inform the next stage of my analysis. As my first astronomy conference, UKEXOM was both motivating and grounding. It was encouraging to see the range of active projects in exoplanet atmospheres across the UK, and to realise how my work fits within this landscape. I had many productive conversations with researchers working on similar retrieval methods, hot Jupiter dynamics, and the 3D nature of atmospheres.

Many of the connections I made here continued at the BOWIE-ALIGN meeting, where I saw how shared goals across institutions are shaping future collaborative efforts, in particular for JWST proposals.
